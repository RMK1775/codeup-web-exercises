How old is the National Park System?
The National Park Service was created by an act signed by President Woodrow Wilson on August 25, 1916. Yellowstone National Park was established by an act signed by President Ulysses S. Grant on March 1, 1872, as the nation's first national park.
What is the origin of the National Park Service arrowhead?
The arrowhead was authorized as the official National Park Service emblem by the Secretary of the Interior on July 20, 1951. The components of the arrowhead may have been inspired by key attributes of the National Park System, with the sequoia tree and bison representing vegetation and wildlife, the mountains and water representing scenic and recreational values, and the arrowhead itself representing historical and archeological values.

Before you head out, check with the national parks that you intend to visit. In many national parks, off-road driving is illegal.

How do I reserve a tour in a park?
You can book some park tours through Recreation.gov such as Pearl Harbor National Memorial, many Mammoth Cave National Park cave tours, Independence National Historical Park tours, and more. Not all parks participate in this reservation service.

Mammoth Cave National Park

  • World's longest known cave system with over 400 miles explored.
  • It is a UNESCO World Heritage site.
  • Underground temps range from mid-50s to low 60s, making it a year round destination.
  • The longest tour is the six-hour Wild Cave tour; available by reservation all summer and on weekends.

Mesa Verde National Park

  • Largest archeological preserve in the U.S. with over 5000 sites.
  • The sites reflect the changes made over generations by the Anasazi, or ancestral Puebloans.
  • Rediscovered some 600 years after their abandonment by two cowboys tracking strays.
  • Artifacts and dwellings date from 550 - 1300 A.D.

Redwood National Park

  • The world's tallest tree, Hyperion, is here.
  • Many of the Sequoias within this park are over a thousand years old.
  • It will currently take 250+ years to recover from the logging done in the 19th and 20th centuries.
  • Redwood root systems can be as shallow as 10 feet despite their immense size.
